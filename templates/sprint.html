Free Sprint Planning Gantt Chart Template

A free sprint planning gantt chart template that puts a whole sprint on a day-by-day timeline — ceremonies, stories and capacity in one view. Use it as a scrum gantt chart template or simple sprint schedule template: download it for Excel or CSV, or open it online to sequence stories, link dependencies and keep the sprint within capacity.

What's included

The template lays out a single sprint from planning to retro, each part pre-filled so you can adapt rather than start from a blank page:

  • Sprint planning — commit the backlog and size the stories on day one.
  • Development — the stories themselves, plus supporting work like API and integration tasks.
  • QA & testing — verification that runs alongside and after development.
  • Sprint review — the demo of completed work. Milestone: sprint review.
  • Retrospective — the team's look back before the next sprint begins.
Sequence matters inside a sprint. Integration waits on the API; QA waits on the story it tests. Open this in the free gantts.app editor to link those relationships — reorder a story and everything downstream shifts, so you can see whether the sprint still fits in two weeks. No sign up.

A 2-week sprint day-by-day

The grid spans ten working days. Day one is sprint planning; the middle days are development and testing running in parallel; the final days converge on the review and retrospective. Laying the sprint out day-by-day makes over-commitment obvious before it happens — if the QA bars pile up in the last two days, you've planned too much. The working-day calendar skips weekends automatically, so a two-week sprint really means ten days, not fourteen.

Ceremonies & story tracking

Scrum ceremonies — planning, review and retrospective — are placed as fixed points so the cadence is visible at a glance, and you can add a recurring daily standup if you want it on the chart. Each story is a bar you assign to an owner, which turns the timeline into a lightweight capacity view: you can see who is loaded on which days and rebalance before the sprint starts rather than mid-flight.

How to customize free

  1. Set the sprint start date and let the two-week grid line up with your calendar in the free online editor.
  2. Drop in your committed stories and size each bar to its estimate.
  3. Assign owners so capacity per person is visible across the sprint.
  4. Link dependencies between stories, API work, integration and QA.
  5. Mark the review as a milestone and add standups if you want the full cadence on the chart.

Scrum tips

  • Plan to capacity, not to hope. If the bars overflow the last two days, cut scope in planning — the chart shows it immediately.
  • Front-load risk. Sequence the riskiest or most-blocking stories early so there's time to react.
  • Keep QA in parallel. Testing that only starts at the end creates a crunch; overlap it with development on the timeline.
  • Protect the review date. Treat the sprint review milestone as fixed and manage scope around it.
  • Update daily. A quick % complete pass before standup keeps the timeline honest and the retro grounded in facts.
