Roadmap vs Gantt chart

A roadmap communicates direction — the themes and outcomes you're pursuing over time. A Gantt chart shows the execution underneath: task durations, sequencing and dependencies. They're often treated as rivals, but they answer different questions, and the best planning tools show both. This template gives you a roadmap timeline you can present to stakeholders, backed by Gantt-level detail your team can actually work from — so the story on the slide matches the plan in the tool.

Quarters, releases & milestones

Each quarter is a container for a theme, and each feature is a bar that spans the weeks it takes to build. Release milestones — the diamonds at the end of Q1 and Q2 — are the fixed points stakeholders care about. Because everything lives on one timeline, a delay in Q1 visibly pushes the Q2 release rather than hiding in a status doc. That's the advantage of running a quarterly roadmap as a Gantt chart: commitments and reality sit side by side.

Roadmap tips for PMs

  • Roadmap by outcome, plan by task. Keep the presented roadmap theme-level; keep the working Gantt task-level. This template lets you toggle between them.
  • Get less precise further out. Q1 can be dated tightly; Q3 should be looser bars, not false-precision commitments.
  • Protect release milestones. Treat each release diamond as a fixed gate and manage scope around it rather than letting the date drift silently.
  • Re-baseline every quarter. Roll the roadmap forward, archive what shipped, and re-date the remaining bars against reality.
  • Show the critical path. When you present, highlight the chain that actually drives the next release so leadership funds the right work.
