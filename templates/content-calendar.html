What's included

The template maps a typical content pipeline as phases, each pre-filled with the stages a piece moves through so you can adapt an editorial calendar rather than start from a blank grid:

  • Strategy & ideation — topic research, keyword planning, and the content calendar for the period.
  • Blog post — draft, edit & design, then publish. Milestone: post published.
  • Email newsletter — write, build and schedule the send tied to the blog post.
  • Social promotion — create assets and schedule posts across channels to amplify the piece.

Your content pipeline on a timeline

A grid calendar tells you what publishes on which day. A Gantt content calendar tells you the whole story: when drafting starts, how long editing and design take, and when promotion kicks in. That end-to-end view is where content plans usually break — a post scheduled for Friday that only starts drafting on Thursday. Seeing the pipeline on a timeline makes those crunches obvious well before deadline, so you can rebalance work across the week or month.

Channels, owners & deadlines

Every piece has three things this template makes explicit: a channel (blog, email, social), an owner (writer, editor, designer), and a deadline (the publish milestone). When you open it in the editor you can color by channel or group by owner, so each person sees their queue and every deadline is a visible milestone. Dependencies keep the sequence honest — social promotion can't go out before the blog post publishes.

Editorial workflow tips

  • Work backfrom the publish date. Set the milestone first, then schedule drafting and editing back from it with buffer.
  • Batch by stage. Grouping all drafting or all design into blocks is more efficient than context-switching per piece.
  • Make promotion a dependency. Tie email and social to the publish milestone so amplification never gets forgotten.
  • Plan a full month at a time. A monthly or quarterly horizon keeps your pipeline full and avoids last-minute scrambles.

Frequently asked questions

What does the content calendar template include?

It maps a content pipeline on a timeline: strategy and ideation, a blog post workflow (draft, edit and design, publish), an email newsletter, and social promotion — each with owners and deadlines you can edit.

How is a Gantt content calendar better than a grid calendar?

A grid shows publish dates; a Gantt shows the whole production timeline — drafting, editing, design and promotion — so you can see workload, spot bottlenecks, and catch pieces that won't be ready in time.
