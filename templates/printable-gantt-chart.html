What's included

Most Gantt charts are designed for screens and fall apart on paper. This one is built for the wall and the meeting table:

  • Landscape by default — Timelines are wide, so the template is laid out landscape rather than forcing you to rotate and rescale.
  • A4 and US Letter — Margins that work on both paper sizes, so it prints correctly on either side of the Atlantic.
  • Writable row height — Rows are tall enough to annotate by hand — the point of printing is usually to mark it up.
  • Readable at arm's length — Task names and date headers stay legible when the sheet is pinned to a wall, not just when zoomed on screen.
  • Filled or blank — Start from a worked example, or print an empty grid and fill it in by hand.
  • Clean PDF export — The online editor exports vector PDF, so text stays sharp at any print size and there is no watermark.
The most common printing complaint is a chart spilling onto a second page. The fix is almost always zoom level rather than paper size: switch the timeline from day to week or month view before exporting, and a schedule that needed three sheets fits on one.

How to customize it

  1. Choose your paper size before exporting, and keep the orientation landscape.
  2. Switch the timeline zoom to week or month so the full project fits the sheet width.
  3. Trim to the top two levels of the plan; printed charts are for communication, not task management.
  4. Increase row height if people will annotate the printout by hand.
  5. Export to PDF rather than PNG so the text stays sharp when printed.

Scheduling tips

  • Fit-to-one-page is a zoom decision. Compressing the timeline granularity beats shrinking the font to 6pt.
  • Print in landscape. Portrait wastes the axis that a Gantt chart actually needs.
  • Hide columns you will not read on paper. Assignee and progress often matter on screen but clutter a printout.
  • Use milestones as anchors. On a printed sheet, diamonds are what people find first.
  • Print in colour if you can. The critical path highlight is the main thing lost in greyscale.

Frequently asked questions

How do I print a Gantt chart on one page?

Change the timeline zoom from day to week or month so the whole project fits the sheet width, print in landscape, and export to PDF. Reducing zoom fixes the problem far more reliably than shrinking the font.

Is there a blank printable Gantt chart?

Yes. Download the template and delete the sample rows, or open it in the free editor, clear the tasks and export an empty grid to PDF.

What paper size does the template use?

It is laid out to print correctly on both A4 and US Letter in landscape orientation, with margins that suit either.

Is the printable template really free?

Yes — free to download, free to edit online, and exports carry no watermark.
