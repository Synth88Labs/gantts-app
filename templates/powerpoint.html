A slide-ready timeline

Unlike a spreadsheet, PowerPoint is where most people actually present a schedule — to a client, a steering committee, or a leadership team. This template gives you a single, well-proportioned slide with a month-by-month header, task rows down the left, and colored bars positioned along the timeline. Everything is a native shape, so it matches your brand and reads clearly on a projector or shared screen. For a walk-through of building one by hand, see our guide on how to make a Gantt chart in PowerPoint.

What's included

  • A 16:9 timeline slide with a monthly (or weekly) axis across the top.
  • Editable bar shapes for around a dozen sample tasks, grouped into phases.
  • Diamond milestone markers for launches, gates and key dates.
  • A "today" marker line you can slide to the current date.
  • A simple legend and a color palette you can restyle to your brand.
Keep it in sync automatically. PowerPoint shapes never recalculate. Build the plan once in the free gantts.app Gantt maker and export a fresh, correctly-scaled PowerPoint each time dates move — no dragging boxes pixel by pixel. No sign up.

How to customize it

  1. Rename the tasks. Click each label on the left and type your own task and phase names.
  2. Reposition the bars. Drag each bar to line up with its start month and stretch it to the correct length.
  3. Recolor to your brand. Select the bars and apply your theme colors; group bars by phase for a clean look.
  4. Move the milestones. Slide the diamond markers to the right dates and label them.
  5. Duplicate the slide for a "planned vs. actual" or multi-phase view if you need more than one timeline.

Tips for Gantt charts in slides

  • Less is more. A slide can't hold 60 tasks legibly. Show phases and headline milestones; keep the detailed task list in your project tool.
  • Align to a grid. Use the monthly gridlines so bars line up precisely — misaligned bars are the fastest way to look sloppy on screen.
  • Label the milestones. Executives scan for dates. Put the date next to each diamond so the takeaway is obvious.
  • Re-export, don't re-drag. When the plan changes, regenerate the slide from the online editor rather than nudging shapes by hand.
