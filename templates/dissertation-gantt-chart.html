What's included

Supervisors ask for a timeline because it exposes whether a plan is feasible. The template lays out the stages a dissertation actually moves through, with realistic overlaps:

  • Topic & proposal — Topic selection, supervisor meetings, research questions and the proposal document. Milestone: proposal approved.
  • Literature review — Systematic searching, reading, thematic synthesis and the written review chapter — the phase most often underestimated.
  • Methodology & ethics — Research design, instrument or protocol development and ethics committee submission. Milestone: ethics approval.
  • Data collection — Recruitment, fieldwork, interviews, experiments or archival work — the phase with the least schedule control.
  • Analysis — Data cleaning, coding or statistical analysis, and results write-up.
  • Drafting & submission — Chapter drafts, supervisor feedback rounds, revisions, formatting and final submission. Milestone: submitted.
Ethics approval is a dependency, not a formality. Data collection cannot legitimately start before it, and committee turnaround is outside your control — so it belongs on the chart as a milestone with everything downstream linked to it. Plans that overlap the two are the ones that slip.

How to customize it

  1. Change the total span — the template runs 12 months; a master's dissertation is often 6–9, a doctoral one 36+.
  2. Move the ethics submission earlier if your institution's committee meets infrequently.
  3. Extend data collection generously; recruitment almost always takes longer than planned.
  4. Add a row per chapter if your supervisor wants chapter-level deadlines.
  5. Keep supervisor feedback rounds as separate bars — waiting on feedback is real elapsed time.

Scheduling tips

  • Build in feedback latency. A supervisor turnaround of two to three weeks per chapter is normal and adds up to months across a dissertation.
  • Overlap the literature review with everything. It is rarely a clean block; keep a thin ongoing bar rather than pretending it finishes.
  • Protect a writing buffer before submission. Formatting, referencing and appendices reliably take longer than expected.
  • Treat recruitment as a risk. If participants are hard to reach, the data collection bar should be visibly longer than you would like.
  • Export to PDF for your proposal. Most proposal templates ask for a timeline figure; the chart drops straight in.

Frequently asked questions

How long should a dissertation timeline be?

A master's dissertation typically runs 6–12 months and a doctoral thesis 3–4 years. This template uses a 12-month span you can compress or extend by changing the end date.

What stages should a dissertation Gantt chart include?

Topic selection and proposal, literature review, methodology and ethics approval, data collection, analysis, drafting with supervisor feedback, and final submission. All are pre-filled in the template.

Can I put this Gantt chart in my research proposal?

Yes — that is the most common use. Export to PDF or PNG from the free editor and drop it into your proposal document, or download the PowerPoint version for a slide.
