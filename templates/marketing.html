Marketing Plan Gantt Chart Template

A free marketing plan Gantt chart template that turns a campaign into a clear, channel-by-channel timeline — content, design, web, paid and PR — all building toward launch day. It keeps every team aligned on what's due when, and what depends on what. Download it or edit online free.

What's included

The template is organized by workstream so each team can find its lane, with a launch milestone everything ladders up to:

  • Strategy & planning — goals and KPIs, audience and messaging, budget and channel plan. Milestone: plan approved.
  • Content — blog posts, email sequences, social copy, video scripts and SEO briefs.
  • Design & creative — ad creative, landing-page design, social graphics and brand assets.
  • Website — landing pages, tracking and analytics setup, QA. Milestone: pages live.
  • Paid & PR — ad account setup, campaign builds, influencer / press outreach and scheduling.
  • Launch week — coordinated go-live across channels. Milestone: campaign launch.
  • Post-launch — monitoring, optimization and a results readout.
Work backwards from launch. Set launch day as a milestone and link asset deadlines to it in the free gantts.app editor. If the date moves, every dependent deadline re-times automatically — no rebuilding the calendar by hand. No sign up.

How to customize it

  1. Anchor the launch date and let the plan flow backwards from there.
  2. Keep the channels you run and delete the rest — not every campaign needs PR or video.
  3. Assign owners so content, design, web and paid each know their tasks.
  4. Link dependencies — e.g. ad creative waits on final copy; landing page waits on design.
  5. Add approval milestones for legal, brand or client sign-off so reviews aren't forgotten.

Campaign scheduling tips

  • Build in review cycles. Creative and copy almost always need a round or two of edits — schedule the review, not just the first draft.
  • Stagger, don't stack. If design feeds paid, paid, and social, sequence those so one late asset doesn't block three teams at once.
  • Front-load content. SEO and long-form content take time to produce and, for organic, time to rank — start them well before launch.
  • Protect launch week. Keep the days right before go-live light so there's slack to handle last-minute fixes.
  • Share the timeline as a deck. Export to PowerPoint for stakeholder and client updates so everyone sees the same plan.
