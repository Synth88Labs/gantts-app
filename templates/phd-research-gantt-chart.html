What's included

A PhD timeline has to work at year scale, not week scale. The template is organized around programme milestones rather than daily tasks:

  • Year 1 — foundations — Coursework, supervisor meetings, literature scoping, methods training and the qualifying or upgrade exam. Milestone: qualifying exam passed.
  • Proposal & candidacy — Research design, proposal writing, committee formation and the proposal defense. Milestone: candidacy confirmed.
  • Year 2 — data collection — Ethics approval, fieldwork, experiments or archival work, plus the first conference outputs.
  • Year 3 — analysis & output — Analysis, paper writing, conference presentations and publication submissions.
  • Writing up — Chapter drafting, supervisor feedback cycles, full-thesis revision and formatting.
  • Submission & viva — Thesis submission, examiner reading period, viva voce and corrections. Milestone: degree awarded.
The examiner reading period and viva scheduling are dead time you do not control — often two to four months between submission and defense. Show it on the chart, because funding end dates and job applications key off the real completion date, not the submission date.

How to customize it

  1. Set your programme length — the template runs about 3.5 years; adjust the end date and the phases rescale.
  2. Delete the coursework rows if you are on a research-only programme.
  3. Add publication rows if your degree is by published works.
  4. Insert your institution's annual review or progression checkpoints as milestones.
  5. Extend the fieldwork bar if your data depends on seasons, cohorts or external access.

Scheduling tips

  • Plan at month resolution, not day. A multi-year chart is unreadable at daily zoom; use the month or quarter view.
  • Put progression checkpoints on as milestones. Annual reviews and upgrade panels are hard gates in most programmes.
  • Overlap writing with analysis. Waiting until analysis is fully finished before writing is the most common cause of a late-stage crunch.
  • Track publications separately. Journal review cycles run on their own clock and can outlast a chapter.
  • Re-baseline each year. Set a baseline at the start of each academic year and track variance so your annual report is evidence-based.

Frequently asked questions

How do I make a Gantt chart for a PhD?

Work at month or quarter resolution across the whole programme, anchor it on progression milestones (qualifying exam, candidacy, submission, viva) and keep tasks coarse. This template gives you that structure pre-filled.

What should a PhD timeline include?

Coursework and training, literature review, proposal and candidacy, ethics, data collection, analysis, publications, writing up, submission and viva — plus your institution's annual review checkpoints.

How long after submission is the viva?

Commonly two to four months, depending on examiner availability. The template includes that gap explicitly, since it affects your real completion date and funding end.
