What's included

A data center is an electrical and mechanical project wearing a building. The template weights the phases accordingly, with commissioning treated as a full phase rather than a line at the end:

  • Site selection & power — Site search, utility capacity confirmation, power reservation, fibre routes and land acquisition. Milestone: power secured.
  • Design & permitting — Concept and detailed design, redundancy topology (N+1, 2N), planning permission and building permits.
  • Long-lead procurement — Generators, UPS systems, switchgear, chillers and transformers — ordered against lead times that now routinely dominate the programme.
  • Shell & core — Earthworks, foundations, structure, envelope and roof, plus the raised floor and containment build-out.
  • MEP fit-out — Electrical distribution, generators and UPS, cooling plant, fire suppression, security and the building management system.
  • Commissioning & handover — Levels 1–5 commissioning, integrated systems testing, IT deployment and handover to operations. Milestone: service ready.
Long-lead electrical plant is the schedule. Switchgear, transformers and generators have lead times that can exceed the entire construction programme, so the order date — not the ground-breaking date — is what determines when the building can energize. Put those orders on the chart as tasks with their own dependencies.

How to customize it

  1. Set the required service-ready date and work backwards; almost every data center date is driven by a customer or capacity commitment.
  2. Adjust long-lead durations to your actual quoted lead times, which have moved substantially in recent years.
  3. Add rows per data hall if you are building in phases and energizing halls sequentially.
  4. Expand commissioning into its five levels once the equipment schedule is known — Level 5 integrated systems testing needs weeks, not days.
  5. Mark power secured, building energized, IST complete and service ready as milestones.

Scheduling tips

  • Confirm utility capacity before anything else. A site without a firm power reservation is not a site, and that confirmation can take longer than design.
  • Do not compress Level 5 testing. Integrated systems testing under simulated load is the only phase that proves the redundancy design works; cutting it moves risk into operations.
  • Overlap IT deployment carefully. Racks and network can start once a hall is commissioned, so sequence by hall rather than waiting for whole-building completion.
  • Watch the generator commissioning path. Load bank testing needs fuel, permits and time, and it sits directly on the critical path to handover.
  • Baseline at permit. Track variance from the point the design is fixed, not from the concept estimate.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to build a data center?

Commonly 18–30 months from site selection to service ready, with construction itself around 12–18 months. Long-lead electrical equipment and utility power availability are usually what set the outer bound.

What are the five levels of data center commissioning?

Broadly: factory testing, component verification on site, pre-functional checks, functional performance testing of each system, and integrated systems testing of everything together under simulated load. The template gives commissioning its own phase for that reason.

Can I use this for a colocation fit-out rather than a new build?

Yes — drop the shell and core rows and keep design, long-lead procurement, MEP fit-out and commissioning, which is where a fit-out programme lives.
