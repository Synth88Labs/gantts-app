Simple Gantt Chart Template

A clean, no-frills simple Gantt chart template for when you just need a timeline — fast. A short task list, start dates, durations and a tidy bar chart, with none of the clutter. Download it below, or click Edit online free and start typing tasks in seconds.

Why start simple

Most project templates try to do everything — resource columns, cost tracking, five nested tabs — and that overhead gets in the way when you just want to map out who's doing what and when. This template strips a Gantt chart back to its essentials so you can capture a plan in a couple of minutes and add detail only if you actually need it. It's ideal for small projects, personal goals, class assignments, and quick client timelines.

What's included

  • A short task list — around 8 example tasks you can rename or delete.
  • Start date and duration columns that drive the timeline bars.
  • One or two milestones for a deadline or delivery date.
  • A clean weekly timeline with no distracting extras.
  • Excel, PowerPoint and CSV downloads, plus the online editor.
Start in one click. The blank online editor is the quickest way to use this — type tasks, drag the bars, and export. Add dependencies and the critical path later if the project grows. No sign up.

How to customize it

  1. Rename the tasks to match your project, and delete any rows you don't need.
  2. Set dates and durations so each bar lands where it should on the timeline.
  3. Add a milestone for your deadline or launch date.
  4. Grow it gradually — group tasks into phases only when the list gets long enough to warrant it.
  5. Export to PDF, PNG, Excel or PowerPoint to share.

Tips for keeping it simple

  • One line per deliverable. Resist splitting every task into subtasks; aim for the level of detail you'd actually track.
  • Estimate in whole days. Round durations to keep the plan readable — precision to the hour rarely helps at this stage.
  • Add structure only when needed. Phases, dependencies and the critical path are one click away in the editor when the project outgrows a flat list.
