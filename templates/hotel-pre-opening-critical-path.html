Hotel Pre-Opening Critical Path Template

A free hotel pre-opening critical path template covering the full countdown to opening day, organized the way a pre-opening team actually works — by department. Rooms, F&B, Housekeeping, Engineering, IT, HR, Sales & Marketing and Finance each get their own track across a 6-month runway.

What's included

Pre-opening fails on the things nobody owned. The template splits the countdown into departmental tracks so every workstream has a visible owner and sequence:

  • Project set-up (T-6 months) — Critical path agreed, pre-opening budget approved, brand standards issued and the opening date fixed. Milestone: opening date locked.
  • Recruitment & HR — Executive committee first, then department heads, then line staff in hiring waves timed to training rather than to opening day.
  • OS&E and FF&E procurement — Operating supplies and equipment specified, ordered and delivered against lead times — the workstream most likely to slip quietly.
  • Systems & IT — PMS, POS, channel manager, key cards, Wi-Fi and telephony installed, integrated and tested before staff training needs them.
  • Licensing & inspections — Fire, health, liquor, occupancy and brand-standard inspections, each a hard gate with no negotiation. Milestone: certificate of occupancy.
  • Training, soft opening & launch — Departmental training, test bookings, the soft opening period and the public launch. Milestone: hotel open.
The commercial workstream is the one that gets cut and shouldn't. Sales, distribution set-up and channel connectivity have to start months before opening, because a hotel that opens with no distribution opens empty. Give it a track on the chart rather than treating it as something to do once construction settles.

How to customize it

  1. Set the opening date and let every track count backwards from it — pre-opening is a countdown, not a forward plan.
  2. Adjust the runway: 3 months suits a limited-service property, 6–9 months a full-service or resort opening.
  3. Add a row per department head so each track has one named owner.
  4. Move hiring waves to sit before training, not before opening — staff hired too early cost payroll, too late miss training.
  5. Mark every licence and inspection as a milestone; these are gates, not tasks.

Scheduling tips

  • Work backwards from opening day. Every date on a pre-opening plan is derived from the opening date. Set it first, then let dependencies push the rest.
  • Watch OS&E lead times. Operating supplies are ordered in hundreds of line items; a single long-lead category (uniforms, custom china, mattresses) can hold up a whole department's readiness.
  • Sequence systems before training. Staff cannot train on a PMS that is not installed. IT readiness is a dependency of training, not a parallel track.
  • Treat inspections as hard gates. A failed fire inspection stops everything downstream, so build re-inspection buffer rather than assuming first-time pass.
  • Run a soft opening with real bookings. Give it its own bar on the chart — it is the only rehearsal the team gets.

Frequently asked questions

How long is a hotel pre-opening critical path?

Typically 3–6 months for a limited-service property and 6–12 months for a full-service hotel or resort. The template uses a 6-month runway you can compress or extend by changing the opening date.

How is the pre-opening plan organised?

By department — Rooms, F&B, Housekeeping, Engineering, IT, HR, Sales & Marketing and Finance — so every workstream has a named owner and its own sequence, rather than one undifferentiated task list.

Can I see which pre-opening tasks drive the opening date?

Yes. Open it in the free gantts.app editor and turn on the critical path — the chain of tasks that determines your opening date is highlighted automatically as dates change.
