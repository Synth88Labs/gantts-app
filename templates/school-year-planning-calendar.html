School Year Planning Calendar Template

A free school year planning calendar template laid out at institution level rather than classroom level — curriculum and timetable build, staffing and cover, admissions and enrolment, term dates and holidays, assessment and external exam windows, reporting to parents, inspections and open events, and the budget year that runs underneath all of it.

What's included

An academic year is planned twice: once in the spring before it starts, and once again week by week while it runs. The template shows both, with the planning phases sitting before day one of term:

  • Curriculum & timetable — Curriculum model and option blocks, subject allocation, timetable build and validation, room and resource allocation. Milestone: timetable published.
  • Staffing & contracts — Establishment review, resignations and recruitment rounds, contracts issued, cover and supply arrangements, induction of new staff.
  • Admissions & enrolment — Open evenings, application window, offers and appeals, enrolment and results-day enrolment, transition days for new intakes. Milestone: roll confirmed.
  • Term dates & calendar — Term and holiday dates, INSET days, the published calendar of events, and the parent-facing communication of all of it.
  • Assessment & exams — Internal assessment points, mock exams, external exam entries and deadlines, the public exam series, invigilation and access arrangements, results days. Milestone: results day.
  • Reporting & parents — Data collection windows, report writing and publication, parents' evenings, and target setting for the following year.
  • Inspection & governance — Self-evaluation, governor meeting cycle, inspection readiness, safeguarding audit and policy review dates.
  • Budget cycle — Funding allocation, budget build and approval, in-year monitoring, and the year-end return.
Timetable build and staffing are one problem, not two. You cannot finish a timetable without knowing who is teaching, and you cannot confirm staffing without knowing what the timetable demands. Schedule them as overlapping bars with a shared deadline, and put the resignation deadline on the chart — it is the single date that decides whether the timetable is built once or three times.

How to customize it

  1. Set day zero at the start of your spring planning term, not the start of the academic year — most of the decisions are made before September.
  2. Replace the exam windows with your own awarding body dates; those are fixed externally and everything around them negotiates.
  3. Add a row per key stage or year group where assessment and reporting cycles differ.
  4. Insert your governor meeting dates as milestones — they are the approval gates for budget and policy.
  5. Extend the admissions phase if you run a selective or oversubscribed process with an appeals season.
  6. Mark INSET days explicitly so training is planned into the calendar rather than squeezed around it.

Scheduling tips

  • Put the resignation deadline on the chart. It is the hard date that separates a timetable you can build from one you will rebuild in August.
  • Protect the data collection windows. Reports depend on assessment data landing on time; a slipped collection point moves the parents' evening, not just the spreadsheet.
  • Book exam invigilation early. Invigilator recruitment and access arrangement assessments both have lead times and both gate the exam series.
  • Schedule open evenings against the application deadline. An open evening after the deadline is a marketing event, not a recruitment one.
  • Keep the budget cycle visible. Staffing decisions made without the budget position in view are the most expensive mistakes a school makes.
  • Plan the following year in this year's summer term. Everything on this chart before September belongs to the year that is still running.

Frequently asked questions

What should a school year planning calendar include?

Curriculum and timetable build, staffing, admissions and enrolment, term dates, assessment and external exam windows, reporting to parents, inspection and governance, and the budget cycle. All eight are pre-loaded as separate tracks.

When should planning for the next academic year start?

Usually in the spring term of the year before, because the resignation deadline, option blocks and budget allocation all land months before September. The template starts there rather than in September.

Can I adapt this for a college or university?

Yes. Keep the structure and swap the exam series for your assessment boards, and the admissions rows for your clearing or intake process — the shape of the year is the same.

How do I handle two or three intakes a year?

Duplicate the admissions and enrolment rows per intake and keep a single timetable and budget track. The template gives you the pattern for the main intake.
