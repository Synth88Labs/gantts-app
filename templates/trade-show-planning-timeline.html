Trade Show Planning Timeline Template

A free trade show planning timeline template counting down to the show — space selection and contract, stand design and build, freight and drayage deadlines, pre-show marketing and meeting booking, staffing and briefing, the show days themselves, and the lead follow-up that decides whether any of it paid for itself. What's included

Exhibiting is a countdown, not a project. Three or four dates are genuinely immovable and everything else arranges itself around them, so the template shows them as milestones rather than as tasks:

  • Space selection & contract — Show selection, budget approval, floor-plan review, space selection at the priority-points deadline and contract signature. Milestone: space contracted.
  • Stand design & build — Brief, concept design, contractor selection, design approval by show management, fabrication, graphics and pre-build.
  • Show services & logistics — Ordering electrics, rigging, internet and furniture at advance rates, freight booking, the shipping deadline and the assigned build slot. Milestone: shipping deadline.
  • Pre-show marketing — Target list, invitation campaign, meeting booking, press and speaker slots, social and website activity in the final weeks.
  • Staffing & training — Stand rota, travel and accommodation, product and messaging briefing, lead-capture training and the demo dry-run.
  • Show days — Build-up, stand open, meetings and demos, daily lead review, breakdown and outbound freight. Milestone: show opens.
  • Lead follow-up & review — Lead upload within days not weeks, tiered follow-up, pipeline attribution, cost review and the next-show decision.
Three deadlines on this chart cannot move: the space selection date that sets where you stand for the next show, the advance-rate order deadline after which every service costs more, and the freight deadline after which your stand arrives late or not at all. Everything else is negotiable. Mark all three and let the rest of the plan fit around them.

How to customize it

  1. Set the show open date and let the whole plan count backwards from it — that is how exhibition planning actually works.
  2. Replace the generic deadlines with the real dates from the exhibitor manual as soon as it is published.
  3. Add rows for each shipment if you are sending stand, product and literature separately; they have different deadlines.
  4. Extend the pre-show marketing bar if you rely on booked meetings; the calendar fills six to eight weeks out, not the week before.
  5. Add a customs and carnet task for international shows — it has its own lead time and can strand a stand at the border.
  6. Keep follow-up on the chart with dated tasks; leads decay fast and an undated follow-up plan is not a plan.

Scheduling tips

  • Book next year at this year's show. Space selection usually happens on site with priority based on tenure and spend, which is why it sits at the front of this template rather than the end.
  • Order services at the advance rate. Electrics, rigging and internet ordered after the deadline routinely cost substantially more for exactly the same thing.
  • Hit the freight deadline with a margin. Late shipments go to the back of the marshalling queue, and your build slot does not wait for them.
  • Get stand designs approved by show management early. Height, hanging signs and multi-storey structures need approval and will be refused on site if they were never submitted.
  • Rehearse the demo before you travel. Discovering a demo needs bandwidth you did not order is a show-floor problem with no show-floor solution.
  • Follow up within 48 hours. The single biggest driver of trade-show return is speed of follow-up, so schedule it before you go, not after you get back.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should trade show planning start?

Six to nine months for a major show, and longer if you want good floor position — space for the next edition is often selected at the current one. This template runs about eight months to the show open.

How is this different from the event planning template?

This one is specific to exhibiting: space contracts, stand build, drayage and freight deadlines, advance-rate service orders and lead follow-up. The event planning template covers running your own event — venue, agenda, speakers and attendees.

What are the immovable deadlines when exhibiting?

Space selection, the advance-rate order deadline in the exhibitor manual, the freight and drayage deadline, and your assigned build slot. All four appear as milestones here.

Can I use this for a conference booth as well as a big exhibition?

Yes. Keep the structure and trim the stand build rows if you are taking a shell scheme or a table-top presence.

Is the trade show template free?

Yes. Free Excel, PowerPoint and CSV downloads, and free online editing with no sign-up.
