What's included

The template breaks the month into four weekly phases, each pre-filled with a representative focus so you can adapt rather than start from a blank page:

  • Week 1 — Plan — scope the work, agree goals and line up what each later week needs.
  • Week 2 — Build — the first block of delivery, carrying the plan into execution.
  • Week 3 — Build & test — finish the build while verification runs alongside it.
  • Week 4 — Launch — ship the work and close out. Milestone: month-end.
Weeks depend on each other. You can't test in week 3 what wasn't built in week 2, and launch waits on testing. Open this in the free gantts.app editor to link those relationships — if week 2 slips, the launch and month-end milestone move with it and the critical path updates. No sign up.

A month broken into weeks

A monthly view sits at the sweet spot for a project that's too long for a daily grid but too short to need quarters. Each week is a column and each phase is a bar spanning the weeks it takes. Because the whole month is visible in one screen, you can see the shape of the work — where the load is heavy, where the slack is, and which week the milestones fall in — without scrolling through a long day-by-day chart. It's detailed enough to plan against, coarse enough to present.

How to plan a month

Start from the month-end milestone and work backward: what has to be true in week 4 for launch, and therefore what must land in weeks 3, 2 and 1. Put the planning and scoping work up front in week 1 so the later weeks aren't derailed by decisions that should have been made early. Keep build and test overlapping in the middle weeks rather than stacking all testing at the end, and leave a little slack in week 4 so a late issue doesn't blow the month-end date.

How to customize free

  1. Set week 1's start date so the four weeks line up with your calendar month in the free online editor.
  2. Rename the phases — Plan, Build, Build & test and Launch are placeholders for your real workstreams.
  3. Add tasks under each week and size their bars to your estimates.
  4. Assign owners so responsibility across the month is clear.
  5. Link dependencies and mark launch and month-end as milestones so key dates stand out.

Tips

  • Plan backward from month-end. Fix the end date first, then place each week's work to land it.
  • Front-load the planning. Decisions made in week 1 prevent rework in weeks 3 and 4.
  • Overlap build and test. Testing that only starts at month-end creates a crunch; run it alongside the build.
  • Leave week-4 slack. Protect the month-end milestone with a buffer for the inevitable late issue.
  • Update weekly. A % complete pass at the end of each week keeps the monthly view honest for reviews.
