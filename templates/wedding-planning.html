What's included

The template is organized as a countdown, each phase pre-filled with the tasks that typically fall in that window so you can adapt a real 12 month wedding checklist rather than build one from scratch:

  • 12–9 months out — set the date, agree the budget, draft the guest list, and book the venue. Milestone: venue booked.
  • 9–6 months out — book caterer and photographer, choose attire, and order invitations.
  • 3–1 months out — collect RSVPs, finalise the seating plan, and hold the rehearsal.
  • Wedding day — the day-of timeline. Milestone: the wedding day.

Your 12-month wedding countdown

Weddings are the classic backward-planning problem: everything works from a fixed date. This template treats the wedding day as the anchor milestone and lays out every task in the countdown window where it belongs. Because it's a Gantt, you can see at a glance whether you're on pace — if it's five months out and the caterer still isn't booked, the timeline shows it immediately. It keeps a hundred moving parts, and both partners, working from the same plan.

Vendors, budget & bookings

The heart of wedding planning is vendors, and this template makes each booking a task with an owner and deadline. Venue, caterer, photographer, florist, attire, invitations — every one has a "book by" date so deposits and payments never sneak up on you. Use the Owner column to split responsibilities between partners (or a planner), and mark each vendor confirmed as it's locked in so your budget and bookings stay in sync across the whole countdown.

Wedding planning tips

  • Book the big three first. Venue, caterer and photographer get booked earliest — popular dates go 12+ months out.
  • Track deposits as milestones. Put deposit and final-payment dates on the timeline so nothing lapses.
  • Leave buffer near the end. The final month fills up fast with RSVPs, seating and the rehearsal — don't over-schedule it.
  • Share one plan. Export the timeline so both partners and any planner work from the same checklist.

Does it help track vendors and budget?
